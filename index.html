What this is

The grief is real.
This doesn't need explaining.

You don't have to justify how much it hurts. You write what's happening — it reads what's actually there. No timeline. No judgement. Just what you give it.

— The house is quiet in a way it wasn't before. You keep looking for them. You keep expecting to hear them.
— You had to make the decision and you keep replaying it. You wonder if you did it too soon. You wonder if you waited too long.
— It's been months and the world has moved on. Nobody asks anymore. But you're still in it.

Who it's for

Any companion animal.
Any kind of loss.

—A dog or cat you had for years, who was part of every day
—A horse, a bird, a rabbit — the ones that others don't always understand
—A sudden loss, or a long goodbye, or the decision you had to make
—Grief that others have told you to get over — but that you haven't, and shouldn't have to
