LOVE GONE

You don't have to explain how much it hurt.

This tool reads what you write about your loss and maps what's actually there — what's moving, what isn't, what you're carrying, what you keep coming back to.

It works for any companion animal. A dog, a cat, a horse, a bird. A sudden loss, a long goodbye, or the decision you had to make and keep second-guessing. It doesn't give advice. It doesn't tell you how to grieve. It reads what you give it — nothing more.

HOW TO USE THIS

1. Write a baseline. Where things actually stand. What happened. What the days look like now. Be as specific as you can — what you keep thinking about, what's hard, what you keep coming back to.

2. Come back when something happens. A hard day, an anniversary, a moment that caught you off guard. Add it as a new entry.

3. The map reads across time. What's moving, what's stuck, what you keep circling. There is no right pace here.

A baseline might look like this:
"She died three weeks ago. I had to make the decision and I keep wondering if I did it too soon. The house is so quiet. I keep looking for her by the door. People keep telling me it gets easier but I don't want easier right now. I just want to say what it's actually like."

This tool is not a substitute for professional support. It reads what you write and maps what it finds — no more. It does not diagnose, assess risk, or produce a clinical or legal finding. If you are in crisis or need immediate help, please reach out to someone who can support you directly.
